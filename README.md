☀️ Daily Check-In App

A flexible, trauma-informed Primary Mental Health (PMH) check-in tool designed to help patients identify how they are arriving today using multiple pathways, not just a 1–10 scale.

🧠 Purpose

This app supports:

Emotional awareness
Self-reflection
Patient engagement
Communication in group settings
Trauma-informed check-in practices

It allows patients to choose how they check in, meeting them where they are.

🎯 Why This Matters

Traditional check-ins (like rating mood 1–10) often:

feel limiting
don’t capture complexity
disengage resistant or unsure patients

This tool offers multiple access points to increase participation and insight.

🪞 About the Parts Check-In

This section introduces the concept of “parts” in a simple, accessible way.

Examples include:

The Critic (judging)
The Avoider (checking out)
The Fixer (trying to solve everything)
The Exhausted Part (burnt out)
The Hopeful Part (seeking change)

No prior knowledge of Internal Family Systems (IFS) is required.

🧑‍⚕️ How to Use in Group
Option 1: Open Choice
Patients choose a check-in style that fits them
Share responses with group
Option 2: Facilitator Guided
Facilitator selects a mode
Group responds to prompts
Option 3: Deeper Processing
Use follow-up questions:
“What stands out?”
“What do you need today?”
“What would help shift this?”
